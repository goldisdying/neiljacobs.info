How to Build a Bitcoin Audience From Zero

Neil Jacobs
· March 8, 2026 · 5 min read

Most people wait until they feel ready. They want a bigger following, a better brand, a cleaner website. They keep waiting. Nothing happens.

Here is the truth. You do not need any of that to start building a Bitcoin audience. You need one thing: a point of view.

Start With What You Actually Think

The Bitcoin space is full of people repeating the same takes. Fixed supply. Decentralization. Scarcity. All true. All said a thousand times before.

What cuts through is specificity. What do you think that nobody else is saying? What angle do you have from your own experience, background, or industry? That is your edge.

I came to Bitcoin with a CPA background and a marketing brain. That combination is not common in this space. It gave me things to say that other people were not saying. That is where my audience came from.

Your background is your differentiation. Use it. Just make sure your foundation is right - knowing why Bitcoin is different from crypto is the starting point for any credible Bitcoin voice.

X Is Still the Bitcoin Town Square

If you are building a Bitcoin audience, you need to be on X. Full stop. This is where the community lives, debates, builds, and buys.

Instagram, LinkedIn, and others have their place. But X is where the real Bitcoin conversations happen. It is where reputations are built fastest.

Pick one platform and go deep before going wide. Master one before you spread yourself thin across all of them.

Post every day. Not ten things. One thing that is worth reading. And combine it with showing up in person - Bitcoin conferences are one of the fastest ways to compound your online presence with real-world credibility. A take, an observation, a question, a lesson. Consistency over volume, every time. And if you want to accelerate your growth, Bitcoin conferences are the highest-ROI complement to your online presence.

Engage Before You Broadcast

This is the mistake almost everyone makes at the start. They post content and wait for people to show up. Nobody shows up.

You have to go to the conversation before you can bring people to yours. Reply to people you respect. Add something real, not just "great point." Ask questions. Get in the threads.

When people see your name repeatedly in good conversations, they check your profile. If your profile is consistent and your content has a clear point of view, they follow. That is how it works.

Your Story Is Your Differentiation

Nobody else has your Bitcoin journey. When did you find it? What changed for you? What did you lose before you found it? What do you see now that you could not see before?

That story is something nobody can copy. Share it. Not once as a long thread that nobody reads. Piece by piece, over time, woven into your regular content.

People do not follow brands in this space. They follow people they trust. Trust is built through consistency, honesty, and being willing to share something real.

The Long Game

Building a real Bitcoin audience takes time. My 109K followers on X did not happen overnight. It took years of showing up, saying something worth reading, and engaging with the community.

But it compounds. Every follower you earn introduces you to people in their network. Every post that lands gets you seen by people who have never heard of you. The curve bends upward if you stay consistent.

Start with one post today. Say something you actually think. See what happens.
